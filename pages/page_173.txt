Familiarity, on the other hand, causes us to use the
plain, natural “I” and the blunt “you,” and it is good for
a man to be able to speak and write naturally. In the
“familiar style” of essays and in personal letters, we write
as if we are speaking to intimate friends in our unbuttoned
moods, and this way of writing has a charm of its own.
That is also why the humorists usually use the plain I and
you (I believe, I met, I know, I doubt, I can’t understand,
you know jolly well, you will never understand, not if you
die in the attempt, etc.)

logical. A modern Chinese young lady sometimes refers
to her father as “papa” even when speaking to people not
her relatives, perhaps out of a desire to appear dainty.
