5.11. Mass-Words: Grain of Sand, Bushel of Rice, etc.—
Besides the things that can be counted and are usually counted, there is a group of things that can be counted, but usually are not counted. Thus we say You eat a great deal of rice / She has beautiful hair / There’s a large quantity of sand. We never trouble to count the rice we eat, or the hair on a woman’s head, or the sand a child is playing
