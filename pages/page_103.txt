3.76. Pure Supposition: Would, Should, Could, Might.— The words would, should, could, might are supposed to be the past forms of will, shall, can and may. But in reality they express an entirely different class of notions. If you put your finger in the fire, what would happen? Your finger would get burnt, wouldn’t it? This is true at any time. We may, therefore, call such a statement a general supposition.
