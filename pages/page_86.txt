3.53. Few, a Few, Little, a Little.—In English, the meaning of few and little is negative, while that of a few and a little is affirmative. When you come to borrow money from me, and I say “I have little money,” that means I am not going to lend it to you. But when I say “Yes, I have a little,” this means I am willing to lend you what little I have. I have little time for you means I have not much time, or even practically the same as I have no time for you. He talks a little English should be translated as 他會講一點英文, but He talks little English means 他英文不大會講. Study the following:—

You have little time left. You’d better hurry up.
I have a little time left. What can I do for you?
He knows a little French, but does not talk about it.
She konws little, but she talks a whole lot.
There is little use talking. He will never listen to you.
Oh, it has a little use. It will make him know that I do not approve.
I saw a few people there, and had a good talk with them.
I saw few people there, as most of them had gone away.
