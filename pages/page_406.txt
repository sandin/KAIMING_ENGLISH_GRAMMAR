The above accounts for the structure of simple sentences.
Interjections or exclamations are practically independent
in their nature. In such simple sentences, conjj. are
chiefly used to join modifiers: “I saw a good and handsome
boy” / “He worked hard and fast.” The subject and
principal vb. remain simple in nature.
