Things, Persons and Personification

4.60. Things and Persons.—A very important distinction in English grammar is that between persons and things. One would think that the distinction between animate and inanimate things (thus putting the animals in the same class with persons) would be more logical, but, apparently, in English, we have chosen to regard man as the all-important thing in the universe and have put him in a class by himself. Things, whether animate or inanimate, may then occasionally be honoured with a “personification” and thus by a figure of speech be put on a level with human beings.

The clearest and commonest examples of this distinction between personal and impersonal objects are the pronn. he, she, him, her for persons and it, its for animals and things.
