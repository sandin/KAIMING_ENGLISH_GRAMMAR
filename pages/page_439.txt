The actual prevalence of monosyllabism in current American can only be fully appreciated from the following examples, where whole sentences or phrases consist of monosyllabic words. E.g., She got into a fit (=rage) / You've got to hand it to him (=acknowledge his superiority or cleverness) / Knock some sense into that guy (=make him realize his situation) / He was the best guy to put over a deal (=a business transaction) / I take off my hat to him (=acknowledge his cleverness) / What a guy wants is a lot of push and grit that will keep him on the go and not see red or fall flat and get scared when some one shoots a pop-gun at you.
