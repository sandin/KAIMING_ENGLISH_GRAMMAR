7.22. Influence of Modesty, Respect, Familiarity, etc.—
As we have said already, pronn. are used for different purposes of representation. Pronn. are changed also, therefore, when the purpose is changed.

Out of modesty, real or assumed, we often try to avoid using too many “I’s,” especially in writing, and substitute for them the vaguer “we,” as in we believe, we have often seen, if we are to believe his words, if we are not mistaken. This is so especially in writing. Sometimes the present writer is used instead, as in It has come to the present writer’s knowledge, etc. for “my knowledge.” In official signed statements, the undersigned (sing. or pl.) is often used with vbb in the third person (The undersigned is, or are, of the opinion, etc.). Compare Chinese 鄙人, 愚, 愚見, etc. instead of 我, 吾意. This, of course, is always formal and may seem at times affected and unnatural. Compare also the “modest” use of one (§ 7.24, “c”).
