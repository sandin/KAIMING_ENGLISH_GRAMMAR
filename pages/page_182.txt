REPRESENTATION

For whomever he met he had a nod. (For any one whom he met, etc.)
For whoever met him he had a nod. (For any one who met him, etc.)

Thus our general conclusion is as follows:—

| Person and Number | Case |
|-------------------|------|
| Agrees with what goes before: I who am, you who are, etc. | WHO | Is determined by what follows: who knows, whom they know, etc. |

Exercise 49. Decide whether who or whom should be used in the following, and show why:—

1. The question regarding who(m) was to blame for the Great War is not so simple as the ignorant people imagine.
2. She kept the secret from him who(m) ought to know everything.
3. It was not he who(m) she was afraid of, but his lawyer.
4. It was not he who(m) was afraid of her, but she who(m) was afraid of him.
5. You need not be afraid of him who(m) you know is a perfect gentleman.
6. She was the girl who(m) I met yesterday.
7. He is a man who(m) they detest.
8. To those who(m) have, more shall be given.
9. He likes those who(m) flatter him and hates those with who(m) he does not agree.
10. He likes those who(m) agree with him.
