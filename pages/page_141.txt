5.26. Natural Plurals.—There are objects which natur-
ally go in pairs (as trousers, scales, scissors), or in greater
number (as measles, ashes, suds), and it is natural that we
use these habitually in the plural form with -s. When
such pairs or sets of things can be easily separated, then
of course the singular is also occasionally used, as a shoe
(but not a trouser), and a wave, a bubble (but not a sud).
All this is very natural. In referring to them as one
thing, we generally say a pair of scissors, a pair of breeches,
etc. Further examples are:—
