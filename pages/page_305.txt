(4) Quite, almost, principally, largely, wholly, partly, etc. Largely means “for the greater part” (大半, 多半), while greatly means “very much.” Principally, chiefly, and largely mean about the same thing. Wholly, entirely, completely and perfectly are used with both desirable and undesirable qualities or conditions, while utterly and totally are usually restricted to undesirable qualities or conditions, and all these words are opposed to partly.
