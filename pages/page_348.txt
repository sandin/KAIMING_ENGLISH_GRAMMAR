11.62. Duration in Prepp.—Prepp., too, are affected
by the distinction between point of time and period of
time. Of those indicating point of time, at and on are
the most typical, while the more common words used to
indicate period of time are in, during and pending. We
say, for instance, at nine o’clock in the morning; also at
sunrise, at sunset, at midnight, but in or during the night.
The use of on is worth noticing. It is selective. Just as
we think of a bird alighting on a tree (rather than another
tree), so we think of a thing as happening on a certain
day rather than another day (especially when we say
Christmas falls on Friday). In this sense, Friday or any
day is regarded really as a point of time in comparison
with other days. So we say on that morning, on that even-
