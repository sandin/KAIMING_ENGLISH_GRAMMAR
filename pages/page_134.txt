With nn. ending in [s, θ, f] sounds, these sounds are often (but not always) changed into [z, ð, v], due to the influence of the voiced [z]. This change in pronunciation is sometimes indicated in the spelling, and sometimes not. [ð] is pronounced like th in this, thee, quite akin to [z].
