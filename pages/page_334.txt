A good dictionary generally indicates whether an adj. is applicable to a person or a thing. Thus a person may be anxious (人焦急), but a thing or a situation cannot (事急 = “critical”).* A thing may be interesting, but generally a person is only interested, unless he is interesting (as an author or friend) to others. A scene or situation may be reminiscent of some former situation (令人迴憶往事), but a person usually cannot be reminiscent, he can only be reminded of that former situation (回憶往事). Again, a thing or arrangement may be satisfactory, but the person is satisfied. The student should form the habit of watching whether an adj. is applicable to persons, or to things, or to both.
