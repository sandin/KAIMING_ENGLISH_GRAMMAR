The Latin comparatives superior, inferior, prior, senior,
junior are followed by to and not by than, e.g., prior to
this event, superior to him in business ability.

10.14. Implied Comparison.—There are a few adj.
which are used only in the comparative and superlative
degrees, and which cannot be followed by than. These
are all words denoting direction: inner, outer, nether, up-
per, and innermost, outermost (outmost, utmost), nethermost
(rare), uppermost. We say the inner circle or the outer
circle, but cannot say “This circle is outer than that one.”
The comparison is implied. Notice that the words in the
positive degree for these adj. are all prepp. or advv.—in,
out, beneath, up.
