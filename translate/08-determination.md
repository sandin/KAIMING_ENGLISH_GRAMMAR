## 第八章：限定

### 8.10. 替代、限定与修饰

When we say this book, that book, my book, the book that you took away, we are using various ways to indicate or "determine" the particular book we mean, and this we call the problem of determination in grammar.
当我们说这本书、那本书、我的书、你拿走的那本书时，我们使用各种方式来指示或"限定"我们意指的特定书本，这在语法中我们称之为限定问题。

Grammatical means of determination always answer the question "Which one?"
语法上的限定手段总是回答"哪一个？"的问题。

Further examples are:—
进一步的例子有：—

- the same book
- 同一本书
- either this or that book
- 要么这本要么那本书
- the other book
- 另一本书
- each book
- 每一本书
- some other book
- 某本其他的书
- the respective books
- 各自的书
- every other book
- 每隔一本书
- any old book
- 任何旧书
- the second book
- 第二本书
- whatever book
- 无论哪本书

It will be seen that there is a close relation between determination and representation.
可以看出，限定与替代之间有着密切的关系。

The pronn. he, you, they, myself, themselves are all representative words, and yet they all help to "determine" which person we mean.
代词 he, you, they, myself, themselves 都是替代词，然而它们都有助于"限定"我们指的是哪个人。

"His book" also helps to answer the question, "Which book?"
"他的书"也有助于回答"哪本书？"的问题。

There is also a close relation between determination and modification.
限定与修饰之间也有着密切的关系。

We say, for instance, that in the red book, the adj. red modifies the n. book.
例如，我们说在 the red book 中，形容词 red 修饰名词 book。

It describes what kind of a book it is.
它描述了这是哪种书。

But the word red serves also to limit the meaning of book by excluding the green ones, the blue ones, etc.
但 red 这个词也通过排除绿色的、蓝色的书等来限制 book 的含义。

It, therefore, also helps to "determine" the book meant.
因此，它也有助于"限定"所意指的书。

Generally, the modified nn. have a narrower or more restricted meaning than unmodified nn.
通常，被修饰的名词比未被修饰的名词具有更狭窄或更受限的含义。

"Northern Chinese" are only one part of the Chinese people, and "humorous essays" exclude essays that are not humorous.
"北方中国人"只是中国人民的一部分，而"幽默散文"则排除了不幽默的散文。

Moreover, determination is only one form of modification: this, a determinative word in this book, modifies the word book.
此外，限定只是修饰的一种形式：this，作为 this book 中的一个限定词，修饰了 book 这个词。

It is only because the determinative words and phrases are both common and important that we study them as a class by themselves.
正是因为限定词和限定短语既常见又重要，我们才将它们单独作为一类来研究。

Thus we may distinguish three functions:—
因此，我们可以区分三种功能：—

- (1) Representation: to take the place of other words (Chap. VII).
- (1) 替代：代替其他词（第七章）。
- (2) Determination: to answer the question "Which one?" (Chap. VIII).
- (2) 限定：回答"哪一个？"的问题（第八章）。
- (3) Modification: to modify or describe the meaning of other words (Chap. IX).
- (3) 修饰：修饰或描述其他词的含义（第九章）。

They are three classes of notions that we have to study, but one and the same word may serve two or three different functions at the same time.
这是我们必须研究的三类概念，但同一个词可能同时服务于两种或三种不同的功能。

### 8.11. 代词的类别

In the old grammars, pronn. are classified in the following way:—
在旧语法书中，代词按以下方式分类：—

- (1) Personal: I, you, it (also possessive forms my, mine, your, yours), etc.
- (1) 人称代词：I, you, it（也包括所有格形式 my, mine, your, yours）等。
- (2) Demonstrative: this, that, which (pointing out which one).
- (2) 指示代词：this, that, which（指出哪一个）。
- (3) Relative: "the man who escaped," "the girl that he loves," etc. (relating to a preceding word).
- (3) 关系代词："the man who escaped," "the girl that he loves," 等（与前面的词相关）。
- (4) Interrogative: who? which? what? (used for asking questions).
- (4) 疑问代词：who? which? what?（用于提问）。

It will be seen that the possessive pronn. my, your and the demonstrative pronn. this, that chiefly serve the purpose of determination.
可以看出，所有格代词 my, your 和指示代词 this, that 主要用于限定的目的。

The relative pronn. serve as often to determine which one as for other purposes of modification, thus:—
关系代词既常用于限定哪一个，也用于其他修饰目的，例如：—

- I know the man who escaped from prison. (Determining which person.)
- 我认识那个越狱的人。（限定是哪个人。）
- I know this man, who is a dirty rascal. (Describing the person.)
- 我认识这个人，他是个卑鄙的无赖。（描述这个人。）

For convenience’ sake, the relative clauses will be studied in the next chapter on Modification.
为方便起见，关系从句将在下一章关于修饰的部分进行研究。

The above division is useful and convenient as a classification of pronn.
以上划分作为代词的分类是有用且方便的。

Regarded from the point of view of notions, however, these pronn. should be studied together with other classes of words serving the same purpose.
然而，从概念的角度来看，这些代词应与服务于相同目的的其他词类一起研究。

Thus, both demonstrative adjj. and demonstrative pronn. serve the purpose of determination, and their distinction is really unimportant:—
因此，指示形容词和指示代词都服务于限定的目的，它们的区别实际上并不重要：—

**ADJECTIVES**                          **PRONOUNS**
**形容词**                              **代词**
This book.                           I know this.
这本书。                             我认识这个。
Take another one.                    Take another.
拿另一个。                           拿另一个。
Both men came.                       Both came.
两个人都来了。                       两个都来了。

The relative pronn. and the relative advv. should also be studied together as serving the same purpose of determination or modification:—
关系代词和关系副词也应作为服务于相同限定或修饰目的的词一起研究：—

**RELATIVE PRON.**                       **RELATIVE ADV.**
**关系代词**                             **关系副词**
Find out the man who said this.      Find out the time when he arrived.
找出说这话的人。                       找出他到达的时间。

### 8.20. 区别："This," "That," "Same," "Other"

Like this and that, the words same and other may be used as adjj. (the same people, other people) or as pronn. (is the same, for others).
像 this 和 that 一样，单词 same 和 other 可以用作形容词（the same people, other people）或代词（is the same, for others）。

The expressions the same as and the same that are generally used as in the following examples:—
短语 the same as 和 the same that 通常如下例所示使用：—

- This is the same one that you saw yesterday.
- 这个和你昨天看到的是同一个。
- This is the same one as that (one).
- 这个和那个是一样的。
- I eat the same food that you eat.
- 我吃和你一样的食物。
- My food is the same as yours.
- 我的食物和你的一样。
- You are the same age as my sister.
- 你和我姐姐同龄。
- Wealth or poverty is all the same (or just the same) to me. (As adj.)
- 财富或贫穷对我来说都一样。（作形容词）
- But I thank you all the same (or just the same). (As adv.)
- 但我同样感谢你。（作副词）

It is always best to use that when a clause follows (i.e., with a vb. expressed), and use as when a simple n. or pron. follows (i.e., when there is no vb. following, or when the vb. is understood).
当后面接从句（即带有动词）时，最好使用 that；当后面接简单名词或代词时（即后面没有动词，或动词被省略时），使用 as。

When there is a vb. with the idea of sharing, we may use with, as—
当动词带有分享的含义时，我们可以使用 with，如—

- I live in the same room with him. (= I share a room with him.)
- 我和他住在同一个房间。（= 我和他共用一个房间。）

The use of another, the other and others follows the usage regarding the use or omission of a and the.
another, the other 和 others 的使用遵循关于 a 和 the 的使用或省略的用法。

- A boy.                 Another boy.           (Indefinite sing.)
- 一个男孩。             另一个男孩。           （不定单数）
- Boys.                  Others.                (Indefinite pl.)
- 男孩们。               其他男孩。             （不定复数）
- The boy(s).            The other(s).          (Definite sing. or pl.)
- 这个/这些男孩。        另一个/其他男孩。      （定指单数或复数）

Another is simply a compound word formed by an and other.
Another 只是一个由 an 和 other 组成的复合词。

We may say, also, some other boy or boys, any other boy(s) and no other boy(s), as we say some boy(s), any boy(s) and no boy(s).
我们也可以说 some other boy or boys, any other boy(s) 和 no other boy(s)，就像我们说 some boy(s), any boy(s) 和 no boy(s) 一样。

No other is followed by than, but different, differ are followed by from, e.g.—
No other 后面接 than，但 different, differ 后面接 from，例如—

- This is no other than the one we saw yesterday.
- 这正和我们昨天看到的是同一个。
- It can be no other person than Mr. Ma himself.
- 这不可能是别人，正是马先生本人。
- This is different from the one we saw.
- 这个和我们看到的不同。

### 8.30. 所有格代词和名词
