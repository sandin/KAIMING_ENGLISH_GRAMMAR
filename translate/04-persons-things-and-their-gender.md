## CHAPTER IV: PERSONS, THINGS AND THEIR GENDER
## 第四章：人、物及其属性

### 4.10. Classes of Things
### 4.10. 事物的类别

In this chapter, we shall study the English notions regarding the classes of things that are expressed by the nouns.
本章将研究英语中由名词表达的事物类别概念。

We have seen already (§ 2.15) that the English nouns may denote visible things (tables, chairs), invisible things (air, noise, possibility, power), or half visible and half invisible things (government, applause, happiness, middle, end).
我们已经看到（§ 2.15），英语名词可以表示可见之物（桌子、椅子）、不可见之物（空气、噪音、可能性、力量），或半可见半不可见之物（政府、掌声、幸福、中间、结束）。

They may denote a concrete thing (table, chair), an action or event (promotion, examination, the fall of a government), or a condition (poverty, danger, prosperity) or a quality (kindness, stupidity, thinness).
它们可以表示具体事物（桌子、椅子）、动作或事件（晋升、考试、政府垮台）、状态（贫穷、危险、繁荣）或品质（善良、愚蠢、瘦弱）。

All these are regarded as nouns from the English grammatical point of view.
从英语语法角度看，所有这些都被视为名词。

To these we must add also the noun clauses, such as That he is dead (is certain), How much he took (nobody knows).
此外，我们还必须加上名词性从句，例如“That he is dead”（他死了是确定的），“How much he took”（没人知道他拿了多少）。

They thus include more than the Chinese notion of 事物 (or “things and events”), for 事物 cannot include the abstract qualities (品性, as kindness, thinness).
因此，它们所包含的内容超出了汉语“事物”（或“物与事”）的概念，因为“事物”不能包含抽象品质（品性，如善良、瘦弱）。

These nouns have been classified in different ways by different grammarians, chiefly according to their meaning, such as “common nn.,” “proper nn.,” “material nn.”
不同的语法学家对这些名词有不同的分类方法，主要依据其意义，如“普通名词”、“专有名词”、“物质名词”。

We shall soon see that such hard and fast distinctions are by no means easy to establish or strictly logical.
我们很快会看到，这种严格的区分绝非易事，也未必完全合乎逻辑。

Still, without trying to put all the nn. into any one system of classification, we can, and Englishmen do, consider the nn. as belonging to different classes expressing different notions.
尽管如此，我们不必试图将所有名词纳入某个单一的分类体系，但我们可以，并且英国人确实，将名词视为属于表达不同概念的不同类别。

The following distinctions must be made clear in the student’s mind.
学生心中必须明确以下区分。

1. Process and Result: heating : heat
1. 过程与结果：加热 : 热量

2. Abstract and Concrete: food food-stuffs / money : coins
2. 抽象与具体：食物 : 食品 / 钱 : 硬币

3. Common and Proper: people : Chinese
3. 普通与专有：人民 : 中国人

4. Collectives: army, clergy, class
4. 集合名词：军队、神职人员、班级

5. Mass-Words: paper, water, milk
5. 物质名词：纸、水、牛奶

6. Persons and Things: writer : inkstand
6. 人与物：作家 : 墨水瓶

Each of these notional distinctions must be studied by the Chinese student, if he wants to use his nn. correctly.
中国学生若想正确使用名词，就必须研究这些概念上的区分。

### 4.20. Process and Result
### 4.20. 过程与结果

In a phrase like the building of a building, or the painting of a painting, the first word building or painting denotes a process, while the second denotes the result of that process.
在诸如“the building of a building”（建筑物的建造）或“the painting of a painting”（画作的绘制）这样的短语中，第一个词“building”或“painting”表示过程，而第二个词则表示该过程的结果。

The one implies a sense of action or motion, the other implies no such sense of motion.
前者暗示动作或运动感，后者则无此运动感。

Compare the Chinese 他所畫的畫 and 佩帶 (=君子所佩, “what a gentleman carries,” i.e., jade, girdle, sword, etc.).
试比较中文的“他所画的画”和“佩带”（=君子所佩，即君子佩戴之物，如玉、腰带、剑等）。

There are many such distinctions in the English vocabulary, and one should make sure whether the noun denotes a process or action, or whether it denotes merely a thing, condition or quality (which may or may not be the result of the process).
英语词汇中有许多这样的区分，应确认名词是表示过程或动作，还是仅表示事物、状态或品质（后者可能是也可能不是过程的结果）。

Compare the following:
比较以下例子：

- ACTION: wedding day, heating, allowing, immunization, differentiation, distinction, presentation, identification
- 动作：婚礼日、加热、允许、免疫、分化、区分、展示、识别

- NOT ACTION (things, qualities and conditions): his marriage (result), heat, allowance (=money allowed), immunity from disease, difference, present (=thing presented), identity of person
- 非动作（事物、品质和状态）：他的婚姻（结果）、热量、津贴（=被允许的钱）、疾病免疫力、差异、礼物（=被赠送之物）、个人身份

### 4.21. Process-Words Denoting Results
### 4.21. 表示结果的过程词

It is very natural, however, that the word denoting a process is often used to denote the result of that process.
然而，表示过程的词常被用来表示该过程的结果，这是很自然的。

From the improvement (improving) of the country, it is natural to transfer the meaning to the improvement actually made, as I noticed many improvements (things added, etc.).
从“国家的改进（improving）”，很自然地将意义转移到实际完成的改进上，如“我注意到许多改进（增加的东西等）”。

And from the destination (destining) of a boat’s voyage, it is natural to shift the meaning to the port to which the boat sails or is destined, as The destination is Amoy.
从“船只航行的目的地（destining）”，很自然地将意义转移到船只驶向或被指定的港口，如“目的地是厦门”。

Because the skin of roast duck or roast pig crackles in the mouth, we now call such skin crackling, and because the lining of a long gown is used for lining the inside, we call it lining.
因为烤鸭或烤猪的皮在嘴里会发出脆响，我们现在称这种皮为“crackling”（脆皮）；又因为长袍的衬里是用来做内部衬里的，我们称之为“lining”（衬里）。

Further examples are:
更多例子如下：

- PROCESS-WORDS: washing, gathering, following, division, distinction, description, information
- 过程词：洗涤、聚集、跟随、划分、区分、描述、通知

- RESULTS DENOTED: things to be washed (this week's washing), crowd gathered (a large gathering), group of followers (a large following), an army unit (the division commander) [師長], fame and honour (a man of distinction), type, kind (people of every description), informed news (information leaked out)
- 表示的结果：待洗之物（本周的洗涤）、聚集的人群（一大群聚集者）、追随者群体（一大群追随者）、军队单位（师长）、名声与荣誉（卓越之人）、类型、种类（各式各样的人）、被告知的消息（泄露的信息）

This change of meaning is very common and offers no grammatical difficulties to the student.
这种意义转变非常普遍，对学生不构成语法困难。

But the student should observe the difference.
但学生应注意其区别。

When the heating of a house is bad, we should not say The heat is bad, nor should we condemn the song because the singing is atrocious.
当房屋的供暖系统不好时，我们不应说“热量不好”，也不应因为演唱很糟糕而谴责歌曲本身。

**练习34.** 判断斜体词是直接表示动作还是非动作：

1. 在威尔逊的第二届政府期间，美国参加了世界大战。

2. 你缺乏想象力（= 想象能力）。

4. 供应受消费（= 消耗）影响。

5. 他是消费病（= 肺结核）的受害者。

6. Fertilization—fertility.
6. 受精—肥沃。

   交流—社区。

   监禁—监狱。

   教育—知识。

### 4.50. Abstract and Concrete Nouns
### 4.50. 抽象名词与具体名词

Abstract nn. are those representing abstract ideas.
抽象名词是那些代表抽象概念的名词。

Thus the Chinese conceptions of “good fortune,” “official position,” and “longevity” (福, 祿, 壽) are abstract ideas (抽象的), while “having a hundred children and a thousand grandchildren,” “being prime ministers at court and generals in the field” and “living to seventy years of age (百子千孫, 出將入相, 年逾古稀) are the corresponding concrete ideas (具體的).
因此，中文的“福”、“禄”、“寿”概念是抽象概念（抽象的），而“百子千孙”、“出将入相”和“年逾古稀”则是相应的具体概念（具体的）。

We may change the concrete idea and still retain the abstract notion; thus people nowadays no longer identify “having a hundred children and a thousand grandchildren” with “good fortune”; they will most likely consider having to educate seven sons and seven daughters with fifty dollars a month a curse and a form of divine punishment.
我们可以改变具体概念而仍保留抽象概念；因此，如今人们不再将“百子千孙”等同于“福气”；他们很可能认为每月用五十元教育七个儿子和七个女儿是一种诅咒和天罚。

The Chinese language is highly concrete in its notions and imagery, and many Chinese students therefore fail to learn the abstract notions of English nouns.
中文在概念和意象上高度具体，因此许多中国学生难以掌握英语名词的抽象概念。

Compared with the Chinese, English abounds in abstract words.
与中文相比，英语富含抽象词汇。

Thus, we have nien-ling (年齡, year-year) for “age,” but the abstract idea of “size” is expressed by the more concrete words ta-hsiao (大小, big-small).
例如，我们用“年龄”表示“age”，但“size”的抽象概念却用更具体的词“大小”来表达。

The ending hsing (性) for abstract nn., as in shen-shu-hsing (伸縮性, extend-shrink-character, “elasticity”), k'o-fên-hsing (可分性, may-divide-character, “divisibility”), is a modern innovation of very recent date, due to western influence.
用于抽象名词的词尾“性”，如“伸缩性”（弹性）、“可分性”（可分割性），是近代受西方影响而产生的新创。

Thus many Chinese students can never learn to say, “The essay is perfect in form and content” (abstract form and content), but must always say “Its form and contents are perfect” (concrete form and contents).
因此，许多中国学生永远学不会说“这篇文章的形式和内容都很完美”（抽象的形式和内容），而总是说“它的形式和内容都很完美”（具体的形式和内容）。

This is often as difficult for the Chinese students to grasp as it is well-nigh impossible for most people to distinguish between the church (concrete) and religion (abstract).
这对中国学生来说常常难以掌握，就如同大多数人几乎无法区分“church”（具体的教堂）和“religion”（抽象的宗教）一样。

They would never think it possible for Jesus to stand outside the church window, calling to the young worshippers inside, “Suffer the little children to come unto me.”
他们绝不会想到耶稣可能站在教堂窗外，对里面年轻的礼拜者呼唤：“让小孩子到我这里来。”

In the following, we use italics to indicate abstract ideas and bold face to indicate concrete ones.
在下文中，我们用斜体表示抽象概念，用粗体表示具体概念。

A rosy cheek is the sign of good health.
红润的面颊是健康的标志。

Eating three bowls is surely good appetite.
吃三碗饭当然是好胃口。

This young man is full of ideas, but he lacks experience.
这个年轻人满脑子想法，但他缺乏经验。

I had once a very funny experience with him.
我曾与他有过一次非常有趣的经历。

We went together to see his uncle and get some money from him, and when we arrived, he told his uncle to go to the devil.
我们一起去见他叔叔并向他要点钱，当我们到达时，他却叫他叔叔见鬼去。

China is backward in communication.
中国在交通方面落后。

She should have more roads, railways and aeroplanes.
她应该有更多的道路、铁路和飞机。

You have too many churches, but no religion.
你们有太多的教堂，但没有宗教。

You cannot have justice, when there are too many clever lawyers.
当有太多聪明的律师时，你们就无法拥有正义。

Our politicians have no statesmanship, and our diplomats do not understand diplomacy.
我们的政治家没有政治才能，我们的外交官不懂外交。

They may be busy the whole day attending luncheons, dinners, and making high-sounding, silly speeches, but luncheons and dinners are not statesmanship, and fine speeches are not diplomacy.
他们可能整天忙于参加午宴、晚宴，并发表冠冕堂皇的愚蠢演讲，但午宴和晚宴不是政治才能，漂亮的演讲也不是外交。

Maurice Maeterlinck is against war in principle (in the abstract), but he fought in the war against Germany, when the latter invaded Belgium.
莫里斯·梅特林克原则上（抽象地）反对战争，但当德国入侵比利时时，他参加了对德战争。

One can worship woman (in the abstract) and hate woman (in the concrete).
一个人可以崇拜女人（抽象地）并憎恨女人（具体地）。

You are against Wu Pei-fu, so you are the enemy of the unification of China!
你反对吴佩孚，所以你是中国统一的敌人！

You are criticizing the present government, so you are a reactionary and an enemy of the revolution.
你在批评现政府，所以你是反动派，是革命的敌人。

You do not love me (the invisible self), you love only my money.
你不爱我（那个无形的自我），你只爱我的钱。

### 4.31. Abstract-Words with Concrete Meaning
### 4.31. 具有具体意义的抽象词

In English, the same word has often two meanings, one concrete and the other abstract.
在英语中，同一个词常常有两个含义，一个是具体的，另一个是抽象的。

Study the following:
研究以下例子：

- ABSTRACT: He has both youth and beauty.
- 抽象：他兼具青春与美貌。

  That's the beauty of the poem.
  那是这首诗的美妙之处。

  Life is short.
  生命短暂。

  We love the teacher for her kindness.
  我们爱这位老师，因为她善良。

  That man has no brains (= He is stupid).
  那人没脑子（= 他很蠢）。

  She is proud of her looks.
  她为自己的容貌感到骄傲。

  He is keen in observation.
  他观察力敏锐。

  With an air of satisfaction.
  带着满意的神态。

- CONCRETE: Our youths (= young men) are corrupt.
- 具体：我们的青年们（= 年轻男子）腐败了。

  Isn't she a beauty (= beautiful woman)?
  她难道不是个美人（= 漂亮女人）吗？

  Many lives have been lost.
  许多生命丧失了。

  Thank him for his kindnesses (acts of kindness).
  感谢他的善行（善意的行为）。

  He has a big brain (= He has a big head).
  他有个大脑袋（= 他头很大）。

  She gave him a look.
  她看了他一眼。

  He makes this observation.
  他提出这个观察结果。

  The fresh country air.
  新鲜的乡村空气。

As abstract nn. can seldom be counted, we may often notice by the use of singular that an abstract, rather than a concrete, idea is meant.
由于抽象名词很少可数，我们常常可以通过使用单数形式来注意到它指的是抽象概念而非具体概念。

Thus, we sometimes say, “Dr. Lim has many systems, but no system,” the second word referring to an abstract systematic habit of doing things.
因此，我们有时说：“林博士有许多体系，但没有系统性”，第二个词指的是做事的抽象系统性习惯。

Similarly, we have the following plurals, which show that they are here used to express concrete ideas:
类似地，以下复数形式表明它们在这里用于表达具体概念：

- Impurities in the water: impure substances
- 水中的杂质：不纯的物质

- The pleasures of an old man: forms of pleasure
- 老人的乐趣：乐趣的形式

- Accused of cruelties to his wife: cruel acts
- 被指控对妻子残忍：残忍的行为

- Several possibilities exist: possible ways
- 存在几种可能性：可能的方式

- Youthful follies: foolish acts, habits
- 年轻时的蠢事：愚蠢的行为、习惯

- Communication facilities: railways, trains, boats
- 交通设施：铁路、火车、船只

### 4.32. A Piece of Folly, a Fit of Anger, etc.
### 4.32. 一件蠢事、一阵愤怒等

Not all abstract nn., however, can be used to denote concrete things.
然而，并非所有抽象名词都能用来表示具体事物。

Whenever in doubt, one can always use another way to express the corresponding concrete idea.
每当有疑问时，总可以用另一种方式来表达相应的具体概念。

We cannot talk of three furnitures, but we can always say three pieces of furniture, and we cannot speak of two infections, but we can say two cases of infection.
我们不能说“three furnitures”，但总可以说“three pieces of furniture”（三件家具）；我们不能说“two infections”，但可以说“two cases of infection”（两例感染）。

对中国人来说，
