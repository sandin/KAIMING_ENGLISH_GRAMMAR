## CHAPTER V NUMBER AND QUANTITY
第五章 数与量

### 5.10. The Notions of Number and Quantity
### 5.10. 数与量的概念

There are things that can be numbered or counted (boy, book, ship, school), and things that cannot be numbered or counted (water, air, fame, honesty).
有些事物是可以计数或计算的（男孩、书、船、学校），而有些事物是无法计数或计算的（水、空气、名声、诚实）。

This applies to all languages.
这适用于所有语言。

It would be nonsense to say "one water," "two water" either in Chinese or in English.
无论在中文还是英文中说"one water"、"two water"都是无意义的。

For things that cannot be counted, however, we can express their quantity or amount, as "much water," "a little water," "a great deal of water," and "a basin (or basinful) of water."
然而，对于不可计数的事物，我们可以表达它们的数量或分量，如"much water"、"a little water"、"a great deal of water"和"a basin (or basinful) of water"。

The difference expressed by one boy, two boys is called number (數), while the difference expressed by much water, little water is called quantity (量).
由 one boy, two boys 所表达的区别称为"数"，而由 much water, little water 所表达的区别称为"量"。

This distinction between number and quantity, however, is not kept in the Chinese notion of to (多) and shao (少).
然而，数与量之间的这种区别，在中文的"多"和"少"的概念中并未保持。

Thus, we say in Chinese 僧多粥少, disregarding the fact that monks can be counted, but congee cannot.
因此，我们在中文中说"僧多粥少"，忽略了僧人可以计数而粥不能计数的事实。

In English, we have to say "many monks" (not "much monks"), but "little congee" (not "few congee").
在英语中，我们必须说"many monks"（而非"much monks"），但说"little congee"（而非"few congee"）。

### 5.11. Mass-Words: Grain of Sand, Bushel of Rice, etc.
### 5.11. 物质名词：Grain of Sand, Bushel of Rice, 等

Besides the things that can be counted and are usually counted, there is a group of things that can be counted, but usually are not counted.
除了那些可以计数且通常被计数的事物之外，还有一类事物是可以计数的，但通常不被计数。

Thus we say You eat a great deal of rice / She has beautiful hair / There's a large quantity of sand.
因此我们说 You eat a great deal of rice / She has beautiful hair / There's a large quantity of sand。

We never trouble to count the rice we eat, or the hair on a woman's head, or the sand a child is playing with.
我们从不费心去数我们吃下的米饭，或女人头上的头发，或孩子玩耍的沙子。

When we want to count it, or express the number, we can say a grain of rice, a piece of hair, three grains of sand.
当我们想要计数或表达数量时，我们可以说 a grain of rice, a piece of hair, three grains of sand。

These may be called countable mass-words.
这些可以被称为可数物质名词。

Then, we have the mass-words that really cannot be counted, but can only be measured by measures of quantity.
然后，我们有那些真正无法计数，只能用量度单位来衡量的物质名词。

Sometimes, we can count the drops of rain and drops of water, but nobody can count the drops of water in a basin or a river.
有时，我们可以数雨滴和水滴，但没有人能数出一个盆或一条河里有多少水滴。

We can, however, measure water, as we can also measure the countable mass-words such as sand, fruit and rice.
然而，我们可以测量水，就像我们也可以测量诸如沙子、水果和米饭这类可数物质名词一样。

Thus we can say a bushel of rice or sand, and a pint of water.
因此我们可以说 a bushel of rice or sand, 和 a pint of water。

### 5.12. Abstract Nouns: Piece of Luck
### 5.12. 抽象名词：Piece of Luck

We have seen already (§ 4.22) that abstract nn. can be made concrete by the addition of words like case, instance, form, piece, bit, act, etc.
我们已经看到（§ 4.22），抽象名词可以通过添加如 case, instance, form, piece, bit, act 等词来具体化。

Thus we cannot count abstract "information," but we can count the "pieces of information."
因此，我们不能计数抽象的"information"，但我们可以计数"pieces of information"。

Also, we cannot count "foolishness" or "malaria," but we can count the "acts of foolishness (or folly)" and "cases of malaria."
同样，我们不能计数"foolishness"或"malaria"，但我们可以计数"acts of foolishness (or folly)"和"cases of malaria"。

Otherwise, abstract nn. are used like mass-words, as a great deal of kindness, much kindness, no little care, no little trouble, etc.
否则，抽象名词的用法类似于物质名词，如 a great deal of kindness, much kindness, no little care, no little trouble 等。

### 5.20. The Plural Endings -s and -es
### 5.20. 复数词尾 -s 和 -es

In English, we distinguish between singular (one thing) and plural (more than one).
在英语中，我们区分单数（一个事物）和复数（多于一个）。

As remarked above, mass-words and abstract nn. have no number but only quantity, and so are not included here in the study of number.
如上所述，物质名词和抽象名词没有数只有量，因此不包括在此处关于数的研究中。

Most English nn. now have come to use the ending -s as a sign of the plural number.
现在大多数英语名词已采用词尾 -s 作为复数的标志。

We may call these plurals in -s (or -es) regular plurals, while other plurals not so formed may be called irregular plurals.
我们可以称这些以 -s（或 -es）结尾的复数为规则复数，而其他不以此方式构成的复数可以称为不规则复数。

This -s ending is normally or usually pronounced as [z].
这个 -s 词尾通常发音为 [z]。

Only after p, t, k do we have the pronunciation [-ps, -ts, -ks], because p, t, k have no voice but only breath, and it is unnatural to put in voice again with a [z].
只有在 p, t, k 之后我们发 [-ps, -ts, -ks] 的音，因为 p, t, k 是清音只有气息，再带上浊音 [z] 是不自然的。

After the sounds [s, z, ʃ, ʒ, tʃ, dʒ], we say [iz] and spell the ending with -es, because it is difficult to pronounce s-z, ch-z, j-z, etc. together.
在 [s, z, ʃ, ʒ, tʃ, dʒ] 这些音之后，我们发 [iz] 音并用 -es 拼写词尾，因为很难将 s-z, ch-z, j-z 等音连在一起发音。
