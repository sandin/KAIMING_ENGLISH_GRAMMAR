## CHAPTER II: PARTS OF SPEECH AND CHANGE OF FUNCTION
## 第二章：词类与功能转换

### 2.10. The Eight Parts of Speech or Word-Classes.
### 2.10. 八大词类

- **Nouns**: dog, book, table, school, John, China, water, sound, work, idea, kindness, strength, anger, danger, order.
- **名词**：狗，书，桌子，学校，约翰，中国，水，声音，工作，想法，仁慈，力量，愤怒，危险，秩序。

- **Pronouns**: I, you, he, her, who, this, those, which.
- **代词**：我，你，他，她，谁，这个，那些，哪个。

- **Verbs**: cut, strike, see, think, breathe, feel, know, grow, be, become, must, will, dare.
- **动词**：切，打，看，想，呼吸，感觉，知道，生长，是，变成，必须，将，敢。

- **Adjectives**: good, bad, rot, red, lazy, kind, strong, angry, dangerous, orderly.
- **形容词**：好，坏，腐烂，红，懒惰，善良，强壮，愤怒，危险，有序。

- **Adverbs**: how, when, why, where, well, poorly, now, never, soon, suddenly, here, already.
- **副词**：如何，何时，为何，何地，好，差，现在，从不，很快，突然，这里，已经。

- **Prepositions**: in, out, above, under, against, toward, with, without, at, during, by.
- **介词**：在…里，在…外，在…上，在…下，反对，朝向，和，没有，在，在…期间，通过。

- **Conjunctions**: and, or, because, although, unless, since, both...and, if, then, while.
- **连词**：和，或，因为，虽然，除非，自从，既…又，如果，那么，当…时。

- **Exclamations**: oh! what! ah! there! look! heavens! fire! help! my purse! what a liar!
- **感叹词**：哦！什么！啊！那里！看！天哪！火！救命！我的钱包！真是个骗子！

The above are the so-called Parts of Speech, or eight grammatical classes of words.
以上即所谓的词类，或词的八种语法类别。

A part of speech simply means a class of words.
词类简单来说就是词的类别。

There are classes of words, just as there are classes of animals or plants.
词有类别，就像动物或植物有类别一样。

Thus the words dog, look, China belong to one class, and cut, strike, breathe belong to another, just as we put rats, rabbits and squirrels in one class, and dogs, wolves and foxes in another.
因此，像 dog, look, China 这些词属于一类，而 cut, strike, breathe 属于另一类，就像我们把老鼠、兔子和松鼠归为一类，把狗、狼和狐狸归为另一类。

### 2.11. Definitions.
### 2.11. 定义

The purpose of a definition is to help us decide what a thing is and what it is not.
定义的目的在于帮助我们判断某物是什么以及不是什么。

The definitions of the parts of speech are to help us decide to what part of speech a word belongs.
词类的定义旨在帮助我们判断一个词属于哪个词类。

Such definitions are not easy to find, and are often as inaccurate as grammatical rules.
这样的定义不易找到，并且常常像语法规则一样不准确。

But practically we do feel there are eight classes of words, and the following definitions will be found generally useful.
但实际上，我们确实感觉到有八类词，并且以下定义通常会被发现是有用的。

We can best decide to what class a word belongs by looking at its grammatical function, i.e., the work it does in the sentence.
我们最好通过观察一个词的语法功能，即它在句子中所起的作用，来判断它属于哪个词类。

(1) Nouns denote things.
(1) 名词表示事物。

A thing may or may not be visible (as table, sound, grammar); it may be living or dead (man, dog, pencil); it may be an action or event (a fall, a flood, a dinner, a meeting), or a condition or an invisible quality (sickness, beauty, cunning, danger, poverty).
事物可以是可见的或不可见的（如桌子、声音、语法）；它可以是活的或死的（人、狗、铅笔）；它可以是一个动作或事件（跌倒、洪水、晚餐、会议），或是一种状况或无形的品质（疾病、美丽、狡猾、危险、贫穷）。

(2) Pronouns take the place of nouns.
(2) 代词代替名词。

Thus in John speaks to his mother, but she does not hear him, the word she takes the place of his mother, and him takes the place of John.
例如，在“约翰对他母亲说话，但她没听见他”中，词“她”代替了他的母亲，“他”代替了约翰。

In Take this book, don’t take that, the work that is a pronoun taking the place of that book.
在“拿这本书，别拿那本”中，词“那”是一个代词，代替了那本书。

(3) Verbs tell what people or things do.
(3) 动词说明人或事物做什么。

(But the words is, can, must, will, have, etc. are also called verbs.)
（但像 is, can, must, will, have 等词也被称为动词。）

There is not one definition of the verb which is perfect.
没有一个关于动词的定义是完美的。

Generally a verb is the important word which says something and gives life to the sentence.
通常，动词是表达意思并赋予句子生命的重要词语。

In a good man, the meaning is dead, for we say nothing about the good man, but in He is a good man, or The man is good, the verb is at once gives life to the sentence.
在“一个好男人”中，意思是死的，因为我们没有对这个好男人进行任何陈述；但在“他是一个好男人”或“这个男人很好”中，动词“是”立刻赋予了句子生命。

(4) Adjectives modify nouns.
(4) 形容词修饰名词。

They tell what people or things are like.
它们说明人或事物是什么样的。

Both in a sick man and The man is sick, the word sick modifies man, and shows what the man is like.
在“一个生病的男人”和“这个男人生病了”中，词“生病的”都修饰“男人”，并说明了这个男人是什么样的。

(5) Adverbs modify verbs, adjectives and other adverbs.
(5) 副词修饰动词、形容词和其他副词。

Adverbs generally tell how, when, where or why a thing is done.
副词通常说明事情是如何、何时、何地或为何被做的。

Thus in He is running fast, He is running now, He is running there, the adverbs fast, now, there modify the verb is running.
例如，在“他跑得快”、“他现在在跑”、“他在那里跑”中，副词“快”、“现在”、“那里”修饰动词“在跑”。

In very red and very soon, the adverb very modifies the adjective red and the adverb soon respectively.
在“非常红”和“非常快”中，副词“非常”分别修饰形容词“红”和副词“快”。

(6) Prepositions with the nouns following them form prepositional phrases which modify other words.
(6) 介词与其后的名词构成介词短语，用以修饰其他词。

The preposition itself always shows some kind of relation.
介词本身总是表示某种关系。

There is no preposition which is not followed by a noun or its equivalent, or which does not form a prepositional phrase.
没有哪个介词后面不跟名词或其等价物，或者不构成介词短语。

In He is in the house, we have the prepositional phrase in the house, with the preposition in.
在“他在房子里”中，我们有介词短语“在房子里”，其中介词是“在”。

But in He is in (meaning “in the house”), the word in is regarded as an adjective, because it does not have the prepositional function.
但在“他在里面”（意思是“在房子里”）中，词“里面”被视为形容词，因为它不具有介词功能。

In the house is a phrase modifying he.
“在房子里”是一个修饰“他”的短语。

In He talks like mad (like a mad man), the prepositional phrase like mad modifies the verb talks.
在“他说话像疯了似的（像个疯子）”中，介词短语“像疯了似的”修饰动词“说话”。

All prepositional phrases are used either as adjectival or adverbial phrases.
所有介词短语要么用作形容词性短语，要么用作副词性短语。

(7) Conjunctions join words or groups of words.
(7) 连词连接词或词组。

In he and I, this or that, the words and, or are conjunctions.
在“他和我”、“这个或那个”中，词“和”、“或”是连词。

In You may come this way or that way / You may come or you may go / I will not come until you go away, the words or, until join groups of words.
在“你可以走这条路或那条路”/“你可以来也可以走”/“在你离开之前我不会来”中，词“或”、“直到”连接词组。

(8) Exclamations (also called interjections) are words which we exclaim.
(8) 感叹词（也称为 interjections）是我们呼喊出来的词语。

We make exclamations when we are frightened, angry, or otherwise excited.
当我们害怕、生气或其他激动时会发出感叹。

He a gentleman! (meaning he is not) is as much an exclamation as oh! ah! what!
“他算个绅士！”（意思是他不是）和“哦！啊！什么！”一样都是感叹。

### 2.12. Grammatical Function.
### 2.12. 语法功能

You see from the above that we determine the part of speech of a word by its grammatical function, or by what it does in the sentence.
从以上内容可以看出，我们通过一个词的语法功能，即它在句子中的作用，来确定其词类。

Of course a word may serve different functions at different times, just as a father may serve also as a family-doctor, or a wife may work also as a secretary.
当然，一个词在不同时候可能起不同的作用，就像一位父亲也可能兼任家庭医生，或者一位妻子也可能兼任秘书一样。

Thus we see the words sleep, before and back are used in different functions in the following sentences.
因此我们看到，在以下句子中，词 sleep, before 和 back 被用于不同的功能。

He sleeps. (as a vb.)
他睡觉。（作动词用）

He talks in his sleep. (as a n.)
他在睡梦中说话。（作名词用）

He walked before. (as an adv.)
他以前走过。（作副词用）

He walked before me. (as a prep.)
他在我前面走。（作介词用）

He had walked a mile before he came to the house. (as a conj.)
在他走到房子之前，他已经走了一英里。（作连词用）

At the back of the house. (as a n.)
在房子的后面。（作名词用）

Back parlour. Back pay. (as adj.)
后客厅。欠薪。（作形容词用）

Stand back! (as an adv.)
退后！（作副词用）

To back up a friend. (as a vb.)
支持一个朋友。（作动词用）

In fact, a word may serve a double function at the same time.
事实上，一个词可能同时起双重作用。

In I bought a new hat which you saw this morning, the word which is at once used as a pronoun taking the place of hat, and as a conjunction connecting up the words you saw this morning with hat.
在“我买了一顶你今天早上看到的新帽子”中，词“which”同时被用作代词代替“帽子”，并且作为连词连接“你今天早上看到”和“帽子”这些词。

### 2.13. Chinese and English Compared.
### 2.13. 中英文比较

In Chinese, most words may serve in different functions without any change of form.
在中文中，大多数词可以在不同功能中使用而无需改变形式。

Thus we use t’ien as a n. in 青天 (green sky), as an adj., in 天資, 天堂 (natural gift, heavenly palace), as an adv. in 天天 (everyday), as an excl. in 天啊! (Heavens!), as a vb. in 失其所天, when speaking of a woman who has lost her husband (literally, “loses what she heavens”), and as a substitute for the pron. your in 天顏, when we really mean your (or Your Majesty’s) face in speaking to an emperor.
因此，我们在“青天”中将“天”用作名词，在“天資”、“天堂”中用作形容词，在“天天”中用作副词，在“天啊！”中用作感叹词，在“失其所天”（指妇女失去丈夫，字面意思是“失去了她的天”）中用作动词，并且在“天顏”（当我们对皇帝说话，实际意思是指“您的脸”）中用作代词“your”的替代词。

In these various functions, the word t’ien itself remains unchanged.
在这些不同的功能中，“天”这个词本身保持不变。

Compare also the following uses of the word pch (white): as adj. and n. in 白馬之白 (the whiteness of a white horse), as a vb. in 自白其志 (to explain one’s mind), as verbal adj. in 得大白於天下 (to have made clear one’s position before the public), as an adj. in the adv. phrase 白晝見鬼 (to see ghosts in broad daylight), and finally as an adv. in 白受損失 (to meet a loss without compensation).
再比较“白”字的以下用法：在“白馬之白”中作形容词和名词，在“自白其志”中作动词，在“得大白於天下”中作动词性形容词，在副词短语“白晝見鬼”中作形容词，最后在“白受損失”中作副词。

Sometimes, we do change the form of the word, as luan for disorderly as adj., and luan-tse in nau luan-tse (鬧亂子) for creating disturbance, or making a scandal as a n.
有时，我们确实会改变词的形式，例如“乱”作形容词表示“disorderly”，而“乱子”在“闹乱子”中作名词表示“制造骚乱”或“出丑闻”。

But in English, as a rule, the words change their form with the change in function.
但在英语中，通常词的形式会随着功能的改变而改变。

E. g., we have admire, vb., admiration, n., admirable, admiring, adj., and admirably, admiringly, advv.
例如，我们有动词 admire，名词 admiration，形容词 admirable, admiring，以及副词 admirably, admiringly。

Hence, the Chinese student of English must learn to feel a sense of the different parts of speech, before he can use the different forms correctly.
因此，中国的英语学习者必须学会感受不同词类的感觉，然后才能正确使用不同的形式。

### 2.14. Sense of Function.
### 2.14. 功能感

There are five points in the sense of grammatical function which must be learnt by anybody who wants to speak correct English without difficulty.
语法功能感有五个要点，任何想要轻松说出正确英语的人都必须学习。

They are (1) the sense of thing (for nn.), (2) the sense of action (for vbb.), (3) the sense of quality (for adjj.), (4) the sense of manner and aspect (for advv. and adjj.), and (5) the sense of prepositional force (for prepp.).
它们是（1）事物感（用于名词），（2）动作感（用于动词），（3）品质感（用于形容词），（4）方式和方面感（用于副词和形容词），以及（5）介词力感（用于介词）。
