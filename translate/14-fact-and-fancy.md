## 第十四章：事实与假设

### 14.10. 动词式：与句子语气的密切关系

The usual classification of verbal moods is as follows:—
动词式的常规分类如下：—

- (1) Indicative: I come / I shall come.
- (1) 陈述式：我来了 / 我会来。
- (2) Imperative: Come!
- (2) 祈使式：来！
- (3) Interrogative: Will you come?
- (3) 疑问式：你会来吗？
- (4) Subjunctive: If he come.
- (4) 虚拟式：如果他来。
- (5) Infinitive: To come.
- (5) 不定式：来。

The indicative mood is used for making statements, the imperative is used for giving commands, the interrogative for asking questions, the subjunctive for stating suppositions, and the infinitive for stating actions without any particular subjects.
陈述式用于陈述事实，祈使式用于发出命令，疑问式用于提出问题，虚拟式用于表达假设，而不定式用于表达没有特定主语的动作。

These verbal moods are but certain forms in the employment of vbb.
这些动词式仅仅是动词使用中的特定形式。
They do not conflict with the "sentence moods" studied in Chapter III, but help to form them.
它们与第三章学习的“句子语气”并不冲突，反而有助于构成这些语气。
The interrogative mood is naturally used in forming an interrogative sentence, and the imperative mood is used in sentences which are commands, etc.
疑问式自然用于构成疑问句，而祈使式则用于表达命令等的句子中。

### 14.11. 事实与假设

Among verbal moods mentioned above, the only one that has peculiar forms is the subjunctive mood (if he come / if I were).
在上述动词式中，唯一具有特殊形式的是虚拟式（如果他来 / 如果我是）。
But this subjunctive mood is important not only because of its peculiar forms.
但虚拟式的重要性不仅在于其特殊形式。
From the notional point of view, it represents a very important psychological distinction between fact and fancy in the speakers of the English language, a distinction which is not observed in Chinese.
从概念角度来看，它体现了英语使用者心理上对事实与假设的一个非常重要的区分，而这种区分在汉语中并未被观察到。

### 14.12. 主观式与客观式

From a notional point of view, we may divide the verbal moods into two classes: subjective (主觀) and objective (客觀).
从概念角度来看，我们可以将动词式分为两类：主观式和客观式。
He came, for instance, is a bald statement of an objective fact; He might come / He would come / if he should come are statements of subjective judgments.
例如，“他来了”是对客观事实的平铺直叙；而“他或许会来 / 他愿意来 / 如果他应该来”则是主观判断的陈述。
In That is a mistake, the vb. is is definitely used to state a fact (although it may involve a subjective judgment), but in That must be a mistake / That would be a mistake / That might be a mistake, the vbb. must be, would be and might be state only subjective opinions.
在“那是个错误”中，动词“是”明确用于陈述事实（尽管可能包含主观判断），但在“那一定是个错误 / 那会是个错误 / 那可能是个错误”中，动词“一定是”、“会是”和“可能是”仅陈述主观意见。

The "subjective moods" may also be called "potential moods" (see §§ 3.71-3.77).
“主观式”也可称为“可能式”（见 §§ 3.71-3.77）。
They cover a wider field than the old term "subjunctive mood."
它们涵盖的范围比旧术语“虚拟式”更广。
For instance, in I hope you will come, will come is really a subjective statement (a hope) although in form it is said to be in the indicative, and not the subjunctive, mood.
例如，在“我希望你会来”中，“会来”实际上是一个主观陈述（一种希望），尽管在形式上它被认为是陈述式，而非虚拟式。

### 14.20. 虚拟式：时态转移

It is necessary to study the forms of the subjunctive mood first.
有必要先研究虚拟式的形式。
We have the following forms:—
我们有以下形式：—

- (1) If I had known. (Past)
- (1) 如果我早知道。（过去）
- (2) If I knew. (Present)
- (2) 如果我知道。（现在）
- (3) If I don't come home tonight. (Future)
- (3) 如果我今晚不回家。（将来）
- (4) If I (should) know. (General)
- (4) 如果我（应该）知道。（一般）

In addition, we have to examine the subjunctive forms of the vb. to be (if he be / if he were / if he was).
此外，我们还需考察动词 be 的虚拟形式（如果他是 / 如果他是 / 如果他是）。

It will be noticed that there is a shifting of tense in the subjunctive mood.
我们会注意到虚拟式中存在时态转移的现象。
The past perfect is used for the past, the past for the present, and the present for the future.
过去完成时用于表示过去，过去时用于表示现在，现在时用于表示将来。
Form (4) will be discussed separately (§ 14.21).
形式（4）将单独讨论（§ 14.21）。

对比以下主观和客观陈述：—

### 14.21. 一般假设：If He Should Have, If He Have

The word "should," as is already discussed in §§ 3.76 and 13.60, is used for stating general suppositions.
“should”一词，正如在 §§ 3.76 和 13.60 中已讨论过的，用于陈述一般假设。
The form it . . . should is probably the one most commonly used for this purpose.
形式 it ... should 可能是最常用于此目的的形式。

If you should ever need money.
如果你万一需要钱。
If there should be (Should there be) a vacancy.
如果（万一）有空缺。
If he should forget.
如果他万一忘记。
If I should die now.
如果我万一现在死了。
If he should become the president.
如果他万一成为总统。
If you should be interested in this matter.
如果你万一对此事感兴趣。
If that should be the case.
如果情况万一如此。
If there should be a war between China and Japan.
如果中日之间万一发生战争。
If some one should say to you today.
如果今天有人万一对你说。
If you should win the champion sweepstakes.
如果你万一赢得大赛马冠军。

As has already been pointed out, these sentences do not refer to any particular time, although sometimes a time may be indicated.
正如已经指出的，这些句子不指代任何特定时间，尽管有时可能指明一个时间。
We may say that these are general suppositions, belonging to the general tense.
我们可以说这些是一般假设，属于一般时态。

Furthermore, should is also used in connexion with lest, for fear, in case, and other subjective clauses.
此外，should 也与 lest、for fear、in case 以及其他主观从句连用。

I shall send him a written word, lest he should forget.
我会给他写封信，以免他忘记。
Lest you should think I was unwilling to help you, I am enclosing herewith some money.
以免你认为我不愿意帮助你，随信附上一些钱。
I am putting the money in different banks, in case (lest) some of them should fail.
我把钱存在不同的银行，以防（以免）其中一些银行倒闭。
He is doing it for fear (lest) people should misunderstand him.
他这样做是害怕（以免）人们误解他。
In case you should need my help, just let me know.
万一你需要我的帮助，请告诉我。

Having made this clear, we may proceed to study the English subjunctive forms: if he come / if he be / if he have.
明确了这一点后，我们可以继续研究英语虚拟形式：if he come / if he be / if he have。
Such forms are not in common use at present in standard English (which prefers if he comes / if he is / if he has, or if he should come / if he should be / if he should have).
这类形式目前在标准英语中并不常用（标准英语更倾向于使用 if he comes / if he is / if he has，或者 if he should come / if he should be / if he should have）。
Because they are out of the way, the usual grammars pay more attention to them than is necessary.
因为它们不常用，普通语法书对它们的关注超出了必要程度。
Such forms may be considered as the equivalents of the forms if he should come, with the should omitted.
这类形式可以被视为 if he should come 形式的等价形式，只是省略了 should。
That is to say, whenever such forms occur, it is always possible to insert the word should in it.
也就是说，每当出现这类形式时，总是可以在其中插入 should 一词。
For example,—
例如，—

If it be said that, etc.=if it should be said that, etc.
如果有人说，等等 = 如果应该有人说，等等。
Although it be the last thing I desire=although it should be, etc.
尽管这是我最不希望的事 = 尽管这应该是，等等。
Lest he hear about it=lest he should hear, etc.
以免他听说此事 = 以免他应该听说，等等。
Suppose that he return you the money=suppose he should return you, etc.
假设他还你钱 = 假设他应该还你，等等。
The court ordered that he leave the city at once, or that he be hanged to death=the court ordered that he should leave, etc., or that he should be hanged.
法院命令他立即离开该城，否则将被处以绞刑 = 法院命令他应该离开，等等，或者他应该被绞死。
He agreed to go, on the condition that his family expenses be (=should be) well provided for.
他同意去，条件是家人的开销应得到充分保障。
The widow was bequeathed the whole estate, provided she remain (=should remain) unmarried.
这位寡妇被遗赠全部遗产，前提是她保持（= 应该保持）未婚。

### 14.22. 假设的不同程度

Attention has already been called to the existence of several subjunctive forms (§ 3.77).
我们已经注意到存在几种虚拟形式（§ 3.77）。
In a simple sentence referring to the present, we could use the following forms:—
在一个指代现在的简单句中，我们可以使用以下形式：—

- (1) If he comes
- (1) 如果他来（可能成真）
- (2) If he come (if he should come, should he come)
- (2) 如果他来（一般假设）
- (3) If he came
- (3) 如果他来了（与现在事实相反）

The question is, how should one distinguish them in actual use?
问题是，在实际使用中应如何区分它们？
In order to understand this, we should know that there are different degrees of supposition, from a supposition which may quite possibly be true (if he comes), to a purer form of supposition (if he should come) and even a supposition which is definitely untrue (if he came for the present, and if he had come for the past).
为了理解这一点，我们应该知道假设有不同的程度，从很可能成真的假设（如果他来），到更纯粹的假设形式（如果他应该来），甚至到明确与事实不符的假设（对于现在用 if he came，对于过去用 if he had come）。
Just what degree of supposition is used depends entirely on the intention of the speaker.
具体使用何种程度的假设完全取决于说话者的意图。

(1) Past suppositions are untrue.
(1) 过去的假设是不真实的。
Past events are facts (see § 13.40).
过去的事件是事实（见 § 13.40）。
Hence whether a statement in the past tense is true or untrue is generally known, and an imagined or supposed event in the past is known to be untrue.
因此，一个过去时态的陈述是真是假通常是已知的，而一个过去想象中的或假设的事件已知是不真实的。
This is easily seen in the following examples:
这很容易从以下例子中看出：

If it had rained last night, the roads would be wet. (It did not rain last night.)
如果昨晚下雨了，路就会是湿的。（昨晚没下雨。）

If I had not stopped him, he would have been killed. (I did stop him.)
如果我没有阻止他，他可能已经死了。（我确实阻止了他。）

(2) Future suppositions are uncertain.
(2) 将来的假设是不确定的。
Future actions or events have not yet taken place, and therefore future statements seldom state a fact, but a wish (I will go), an obligation (I have to go), a possibility (I may go), or an expectation (I shall go).
未来的行动或事件尚未发生，因此关于未来的陈述很少陈述事实，而是表达愿望（我愿意去）、义务（我必须去）、可能性（我可能去）或预期（我将会去）。
In any case, there is almost always a strong subjective element (see § 13.30).
无论如何，几乎总是存在强烈的主观因素（见 § 13.30）。
The subjunctive form (if it rains tomorrow: also general tense-form, if it should rain tomorrow) always implies an uncertainty.
虚拟形式（如果明天下雨：以及一般时态形式，如果明天应该下雨）总是暗示着不确定性。

(3) 现在的假设各不相同。
The most common form used is the ordinary indicative: if he comes.
最常用的形式是普通的陈述式：如果他来。
